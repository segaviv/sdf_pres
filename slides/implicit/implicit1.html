Drawing shapes

A shape is represented as an implicit function

\[ f(x)=\begin{cases} <0 & inside\\ 0 & boundary \\ >0 & outide \end{cases} \]

For instance, a circle will be: \[ f(x)=\sqrt{x^2+y^2}-r = \left \| \overrightarrow{v} \right \|_2 - r \]

Is it the only implicit representation for a cricle?

Nope, $\space g(x) = 5 f(x)$ and $\space g(x) = e^{\frac{\left \| \overrightarrow{v} \right \|_2}{r} - 1} - 1$ are also implicit representations of a circle.
